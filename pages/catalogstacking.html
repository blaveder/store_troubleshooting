Identify the unwanted percentage


The first thing we want to do is establish the known discount. For the example below, the shop is showing a catalog discount of 70% on a sale that should show 40%




  1. Identify the known and correct catalog discount (40%) and locate that number in the left column.

  2. Determine the stacking percentage
    • Our known discount is 40%. Located on the left we follow that to the incorrect discount we are getting (70%). This is highlighted in red.
    • We then follow the incorrect discount up and get our stacking percentage (yellow).
    • In this instance, the stacking percentage is 50% (40% stacking with 50% is 70%).




  3. Now that we have the stacking discount (50%), our next task is to find what active rules or categories have a 50% catalog discount and see if the product is common between them. Click on one of the following links to search categories or catalog rules

  • Click Below "Catalog Rules Lookup" link below
  • search for the day you are troubleshooting
  • Search all rules with "50%" (stacking amount) in the title
  • Make necessary corrections
  • Click Below "Active Category Lookup" link below
  • search active categories that give 50% (stacking %) discounts
  • Remove affected items from category or PA sale
